Hike of the Month: Rattlesnake Loop Access

A short local access walk near the eastern side of Rattlesnake Mountain.

By Civilization Tri-Cities

June 2026

Rattlesnake Loop Access is small, but that is part of what makes it interesting. The access route is short, exposed, and easy to overlook, yet it opens into a broader network of dryland trails, ridge views, and shrub-steppe terrain near Rattlesnake Mountain.

Why it’s interesting

This is less of a destination hike and more of a gateway. The access point gives a quick look at the open hills east of Rattlesnake Mountain and connects into a larger trail system for longer routes.

What it feels like

Expect open hills, dry grasses, wide sky, trailhead signage, and a strong edge-of-the-Basin feeling. The landscape is exposed and minimal, but the views and sense of space are the point.

Trail Snapshot

Rattlesnake Loop Access is short — about 0.4 miles, ranging from roughly 470 feet to 555 feet in elevation.

Quick Notes

  • Exposed terrain
  • Little to no shade
  • Bring water
  • Check access, fee, and signage before heading out
  • Good for a short exploratory walk
