The Three Landscapes of the Basin

The Tri-Cities region can be understood through three overlapping landscapes: the native shrub-steppe, the irrigated agricultural grid, and the expanding suburban edge.

Each layer represents a different way of organizing land. The shrub-steppe reflects ecological reality. Irrigation reflects engineering and agricultural ambition. Suburban development reflects current housing patterns and cultural preference.

Seen together, they form a readable map of civilization in the Columbia Basin.
