[Street Name] is the kind of street that helps explain how this part of the Tri-Cities was built. Its homes, setbacks, pavement width, street trees, and overall rhythm reveal a particular moment in the region’s development.

Some streets stand out because they are dramatic. Others are interesting because they are ordinary in a very specific way. This one offers clues about local planning habits, neighborhood character, and the visual language of everyday residential life in the Columbia Basin.

Looking closely at a single street can say a surprising amount about scale, taste, age, maintenance, and the relationship between private property and public space. Street Notes is a recurring feature about those small but telling details.
