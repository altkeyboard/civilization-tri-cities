\# The Large House, Small Lot Paradox



In newer subdivisions across the Tri-Cities, houses often occupy most of the lot. Many are tall, wide, and garage-forward, while the backyard is reduced to a narrow strip of fenced space. The result is a strange mismatch between the scale of the house and the scale of the land around it.



This is especially noticeable in a region surrounded by open land. The paradox is not scarcity in the geographic sense, but the way land is divided, marketed, and standardized within the subdivision itself.



In these neighborhoods, the house becomes the dominant object and the yard becomes secondary. Outdoor space still exists, but often as a buffer rather than a meaningful extension of the home.
